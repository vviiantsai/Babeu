芒種

Mángzhòng
Grain in Ear

the tree at the window · 芒種 · summer 2026

There is a particular weight to the heat of 芒種. Not the sudden shock of 大暑 that will come later — something more considered. The air holds moisture differently now, like cloth that has absorbed water but not yet shown it. The season is pregnant with itself. Everything is at maximum green. The tree outside is fully present in a way it won't be for much longer — the leaves doing their work without announcing it.

芒 means the awn of a grain — that fine filament at the tip of the seed head. 種 means to sow, but also seed itself: potential in its most condensed form. Grain in ear. The agricultural moment: the grain is ripening and beginning to carry its own weight. Not yet harvest. The fullness just before.

In the TCM calendar, this is the peak of the Fire element. Heart and Small Intestine meridians become most active. The body is already working hard to process the heat, and damp-heat begins to accumulate — the characteristic heaviness of a pre-summer humid day, the slight difficulty of thinking clearly, the desire to move slowly and eat lightly. You don't fight this. You move with it. You eat toward it.

節氣食記

The congee was made before noon on a day when this felt like the only reasonable response to existing. Duck, because duck moves through damp-heat in a way that chicken cannot. Its thermal nature neutral to slightly cooling, its effect on the body quieting rather than rousing. Duck clears without depleting. It has a quality of patience.

White rice dissolved almost entirely into the broth — less porridge, more suspension. The grain of the season, prepared as medicine. You are not eating the grain at its peak. You are eating it softened, opened, made completely available to the body. Congee as philosophy: the hard thing made gentle.

Ginger for the digestion to stay warm within the cooling. The paradox of Chinese medicine is that you cannot simply cool the body when it is hot. You must cool it while protecting the Spleen's digestive fire simultaneously — the Spleen is easily injured by cold and damp, and if you suppress the heat without supporting the Spleen, you create a different problem. So: cooling duck, spleen-protective congee, ginger to mediate between them. Three green onions at the end, when the heat is already off, when they are still bright.

I ate it at the window. The tree was occupied with itself. There was nothing else to attend to.

食方
recipe
鴨肉粥
Duck Congee
serves 2 · approximately 1 hour 15 minutes
ingredients
  • duck legs, skin-on 300g (2 legs)
  • white japonica rice 100g (½ cup)
  • fresh ginger, thin slices 30g (6–8 slices)
  • green onion stalks 3 stalks
  • cold water 1.2 litres
  • sea salt, white pepper to finish
method
  1. Rinse the rice once and drain. Set aside.
  2. Bring a small pot of water to a boil. Add the duck legs and ginger slices. Blanch for 3 minutes. Discard the water entirely — this removes the first impurities and begins to open the meat.
  3. In a heavy pot or clay donabe, combine the blanched duck, rice, ginger, and 1.2 litres of cold water. Bring to a boil over medium heat, skimming any foam that rises.
  4. Reduce to a low simmer, partially covered. Cook for 55–65 minutes, stirring occasionally to prevent the rice from sticking, until the rice has fully dissolved into a glossy, thickened broth and the duck is tender enough to pull apart easily.
  5. Lift out the duck legs. Shred the meat from the bone with two forks — it should give without resistance. Discard the skin if you prefer, or return it. Return the shredded meat to the pot.
  6. Season with sea salt and a small amount of white pepper. Taste. Ladle into bowls. Scatter the green onion over the top — sliced thin at an angle, or rough-chopped. Nothing else.
本草注記 · TCM notes

Duck: neutral to slightly cooling in thermal nature; affinity for the Lung and Kidney channels; clears damp-heat, tonifies yin, nourishes blood. It cools without depleting. In 芒種, when the body is working to process both heat and the accumulating dampness of pre-summer, duck offers cooling that doesn't injure the Spleen. The ginger slices (warming, pungent, Spleen and Stomach affinity) protect the digestive fire while the cooling properties of duck and the lightness of the congee base allow the Spleen to continue its work without stagnation. This is not a complicated formula. It is the logic of balance, applied to a pot on a hot morning.

After eating, the apartment was very quiet. The tree continued. There are things that are best understood by doing them at the right moment — not understood in advance, not explained after. The congee at the window during 芒種 is one of them. The season is its own argument.
